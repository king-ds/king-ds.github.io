Using Social Network Analysis to Lessen the Risk of Churning¶

Quick Introduction¶

An employee has a freedom to decide whether they will stay or leave the company. Employee churning have different factors affecting their decision making, for instance, work environment, company resources, salary, distance from home, organization competitors and many more. Occurence of employee churn is a huge problem for most organizations because it may cause a negative impact to the company.

Many business organizations utilize social media platform where they can quickly and easily post a relevant announcements to all, send an important message to different co-workers that belong to distinct departments and interact with other employees by liking, commenting, and sharing their post. This could also help the company to boost their productivity by making this as a collaboration tool to help them work together more efficiently.

In this notebook, we will analyze the cause of employee churn through the use of Social Network Analysis (SNA), it is a method used for investigating the social structures, which is under of graph theory. The data used in this blog was already cleaned and scraped from the social media platform; Workplace, it is a work collaboration platform and intended only for enterprise. In addition, it was developed by Facebook Inc., which has a similar functionalities from Facebook. For more information about workplace visit this Link.

Why do we need these two data?¶

In order to formulate a graph, we need to think of vertices and edges first (some of the textbooks they refer vertices as nodes and edges as links). In this case, our vertices will be the employees who have Workplace account. While the edges are the connections between the two employees who either give reactions to the post or receive reactions from his own post. Example, supposed I've share something about machine learning and my co-worker Benjamin liked it, and then the connection between me and Benjamin are created, if you like other post or received a like from other co-workers, connection will established. Thus, we need these two data because we are going to mapped the status of the employees (churn data) to their activities and engagement in Workplace (post with reactions data).

Now, let's proceed building the graph!

Create an emtpy directed graph then add the vertices and edges. Remember that the vertices are the employees, and edges are the employees who reacted to the post.

Initial Graph¶

Upon adding vertices and edges to the graph, we can now start visualizing the graph.

After observing, the vertices seems concentrated at the center of the graph. Basically, the centered-vertices had collected more edges than to the outered-vertices. The vertices with lower amount of edges implies less interaction while more edges represent more interactions. However, vertices without edges (simply called isolated vertices) represent the users who didn't create and share any post and at the same time didn't give any reactions to other co-workers' post. This graph doesn't make sense at first since our goal is to determine the reason of churning. Now, let's dive deeper by combining the churn data to the graph.
